About Me

Thanks for stopping by! I am a young geographer dedicated to the study of our planet (in all its beauty and tragedy) through the use of spatial data and geo-visualization.

As a graduate of a BA in from the Concordia Department of Geography, Planning and the Environment in Human Environment and a minor in Geospatial Technologies, I worked alongside Dr. Alex Lesnikowski at the Concordia Climate Change Adaptation Lab as a research assistant, conducting a systematic literature review on climate change adaptation in residential housing systems.

From there I moved out east to Nova Scotia where I obtained a Graduate Certificate in Remote Sensing from the Centre of Geographic Sciences in 2022.

Between 2022 to 2026, I worked at GHGSat, a Montréal-based company using a constellation of satellites to monitor methane emissions from space. During this time I led several large-scale geospatial and data-viz projects for the company including:

  • Development of a QGIS plugin to automate image processing and methane detection saving our team an estimated 125 operations working hours per week. (pyQGIS)
  • Definition and data visualization design for customer-facing reports on site-level methane emission detections, trends, global and regional statistics and maps. (R, PowerBI, OpenLayers)
  • Various dashboards for tracking weekly/monthly customer image acquisition progress (Python, Grafana)
  • Satellite constellation coverage modelling. (QGIS, Python, PostgreSQL)

When I'm not finicking over the colour palette of a map, I can be found x-country skiing, cooking, watching my beloved Habs, rollerblading, and dreaming of opening an oyster farm on the coast somewhere. Shoot me a message if you want to get in touch!
